The Digital Battlefield

Cyberspace is a man-made domain. Unlike air, land, sea, and space, it is entirely created and controlled by human interaction and technology.

This domain has unique properties that challenge traditional intelligence models.

Key Properties of Cyberspace

  • Speed: Attacks can occur at the speed of light across the globe.
  • Low Cost: Entering the cyber domain requires minimal resources compared to physical domains.
  • Anonymity: Attribution (knowing who did it) is often difficult and time-consuming.
  • No Boundaries: Geographic and national borders are largely irrelevant in cyber operations.

The Adversary's Advantage

In many cases, the attacker has the advantage. They only need to find one vulnerability, while the defender must protect everything.

CTI helps level the playing field by identifying patterns in an attacker's behavior (TTPs).
