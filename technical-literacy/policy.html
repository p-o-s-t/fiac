Incident Response Plan (IRP)

The IRP is a set of instructions to help IT staff detect, respond to, and recover from network security incidents:

  • Preparation: Establishing the IR team and tools.
  • Identification: Determining if an incident has occurred.
  • Containment: Limiting the scope and impact of the incident.
  • Eradication: Removing the threat from the environment.
  • Recovery: Restoring systems to normal operation.
  • Lessons Learned: Improving future responses.

Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

The BCP ensures that essential business functions can continue during and after a disaster. It is a broad plan that encompasses more than just IT:

  • Critical Functions: Identifying what processes are vital for survival.
  • Redundancy: Establishing secondary locations or personnel.
  • Communication: Ensuring key stakeholders are kept informed.

Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

A DRP is a subset of the BCP focused on restoring the IT infrastructure and systems after a disaster:

  • RTO (Recovery Time Objective): The maximum acceptable time for a system to be down.
  • RPO (Recovery Point Objective): The maximum acceptable amount of data loss (measured in time).
  • Backups: Regular, tested copies of critical data.

The Analyst's Role

CTI analysts play a vital role in supporting policy development and incident response:

  • Threat Intelligence: Informing the IRP with knowledge of current actor TTPs.
  • Risk Assessment: Helping prioritize resources for the BCP based on the threat landscape.
  • Post-Incident Analysis: Providing context for the "Lessons Learned" phase of the IRP.
