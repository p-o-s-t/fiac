Incident Response Plans (IRP)

An **IRP** is a structured document outlining how an organization detects, responds to, and recovers from cyber incidents, following standard frameworks like **NIST SP 800-61 r2**:

  • 1. Preparation: Developing response tools, setting up communication lines, and training personnel.
  • 2. Detection & Analysis: Monitoring alerts, analyzing vectors, and scoping the extent of the breach.
  • 3. Containment, Eradication & Recovery: Isolating systems (containment), removing threats/backdoors (eradication), and restoring services (recovery).
  • 4. Post-Incident Activity: Conducting lessons-learned meetings to update playbooks and close security gaps.

Business Continuity Planning (BCP)

A **BCP** coordinates the broad operational strategy of the business during a disruptive event (e.g., active ransomware breakout, database wipe):

  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): Evaluating which business functions are critical (such as transactional gateways) vs. optional, establishing recovery timelines.
  • Redundancy & Coordination: Setting up backup office locations, emergency contacts, and secondary systems to maintain essential services.

Disaster Recovery Plans (DRP)

A **DRP** is a subset of the BCP focusing specifically on the recovery of critical IT infrastructure and data databases following a major incident:

  • RTO (Recovery Time Objective): The maximum duration systems can remain offline before causing severe business damage.
  • RPO (Recovery Point Objective): The maximum age of files that must be recovered from backup storage (defining data loss tolerance).
  • 3-2-1 Backup Rule: Keeping 3 copies of data, across 2 different media types, with 1 copy stored offsite (utilizing immutable backups).

Compliance & Governance Frameworks

Organizations deploy compliance policies to enforce security controls, audit trails, and data protection rules:

  • NIST CSF (Cybersecurity Framework): Structure mapping controls across 5 core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
  • Standards & Regulations: Aligning security policies with ISO 27001, SOC 2 audits, or legal mandates like GDPR, HIPAA, and CCPA.

The CTI Analyst's Role in Governance

Threat intelligence provides the empirical basis that keeps security policies and plans aligned with reality:

  • Playbook Hardening: Providing threat actor TTP inputs to incident response teams to build realistic containment playbooks.
  • Risk-Informed BIA: Helping GRC officers assess which business critical systems are most likely to be targeted based on industry victimology.
