Operations Roles

The Operations team is the front line of defense, monitoring systems and responding to immediate threats:

  • SOC Analysts (Tiers 1-3): Monitor security alerts, triage events, and perform initial investigations.
  • Incident Responders (IR): Specialized personnel who lead the response to active security breaches and containment.
  • Vulnerability Management: Identify, prioritize, and manage the remediation of security weaknesses in systems and software.

Specialist Roles

Specialists provide deep technical expertise in specific domains of cybersecurity:

  • Digital Forensics (DFIR): Analyze digital evidence to reconstruct events, identify data exfiltration, and support legal or internal investigations.
  • Malware Researchers: Reverse-engineer malicious code to understand its functionality, origin, and indicators of compromise.
  • Threat Hunters: Proactively search through networks and logs to find hidden threats that have bypassed automated security controls.

Architecture & GRC

These roles focus on long-term strategy, system design, and organizational compliance:

  • Security Architects: Design secure system architectures and ensure security requirements are integrated into business processes.
  • GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance): Manage organizational risk, ensure compliance with laws/regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA), and develop security policies.
  • Security Engineers: Build and maintain the security infrastructure, such as firewalls, SIEMs, and EDR systems.

The CTI Analyst

The Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Analyst acts as the "glue" that connects these disparate roles:

  • Dissemination: Sharing actionable intelligence with Operations for better detection and IR for faster containment.
  • Strategic Guidance: Helping Architecture and GRC prioritize investments based on the actual threat landscape.
  • Feedback Loop: Taking findings from Malware Researchers and Forensics to improve overall organizational knowledge.
