Operations Roles

The **Operations** team runs the daily tactical defenses of the organization, continuously triaging events and responding to security alerts:

  • SOC Analysts (Tiers 1-3): Monitor real-time security alerts. Tier 1 performs initial triaging, Tier 2 runs deep investigations, and Tier 3 leads advanced analysis.
  • Incident Responders (IR): The tactical fire-fighters who deploy containment playbooks, isolate hosts, and lead eradication processes during active breaches.
  • Vulnerability Management: Program managers responsible for scanning internal networks, cataloging software assets, and overseeing patching schedules.

Specialist Security Roles

**Specialists** provide deep, dedicated engineering and scientific expertise in specific sub-domains of cybersecurity:

  • Digital Forensics (DFIR): Reconstructing events post-incident by extracting system artifacts, reviewing registry changes, and identifying file exfiltration.
  • Malware Researchers: Reversing malicious binaries (PE, ELF, Mach-O) in sandboxes and debuggers to document execution logic and extract IoCs.
  • Threat Hunters: Proactively searching through SIEM databases and EDR telemetry to hunt down stealthy actors who bypass automated alert rules.

Strategy, Architecture, & GRC

These strategic roles focus on the long-term design, tooling, policy compliance, and overall risk management of the organization:

  • Security Architects: Designing secure system networks, cloud models, and ensuring that software builds follow security-by-design principles.
  • Security Engineers: Deploying and tuning security tools, maintaining firewall configs, EDR scripts, and central SIEM platforms.
  • GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance): Overseeing third-party vendor risks, maintaining policies, and ensuring compliance with regulatory bodies (GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2).

The CTI Analyst Interface

The **Cyber Threat Intelligence Analyst** acts as the central information highway, feeding actionable intelligence to every role:

  • SOC & IR support: Supplying tactical profiles and C2 domain lists to optimize SIEM query speeds and speed up IR isolation decisions.
  • GRC & Architecture Advising: Helping architects prioritize network segmentations based on the actual threat groups targeting their sector.
  • Collaborative Loops: Receiving binary samples from IR and forensics, and feeding back decoded configurations to security engineers.

The NIST NICE Framework

The **NIST NICE Framework v2.2.0** provides a standardized taxonomy mapping cybersecurity jobs to specific workforce roles and capabilities:

  • Threat Analysis (PD-WRL-006): The official NICE work role mapping to CTI analysts, defining competencies in threat modeling and database correlation.
  • TKS Statements: Defining exact Task (T), Knowledge (K), and Skill (S) requirements needed to fulfill each work role (e.g., K0857: malware analysis).
