The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a fundamental model for information security:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring data is only accessible to authorized users. Threats include data breaches and theft.
  • Integrity: Ensuring data remains accurate and unaltered. Threats include unauthorized modifications or tampering.
  • Availability: Ensuring data and systems are accessible when needed. Threats include DDoS attacks and hardware failure.

The DAD Triad

The DAD Triad represents the direct opposites of the CIA triad, often used to describe the impact of a security incident:

  • Disclosure (vs Confidentiality): Unauthorized exposure of sensitive information.
  • Alteration (vs Integrity): Unauthorized modification of data or system settings.
  • Destruction (vs Availability): Data or systems are deleted, corrupted, or otherwise rendered unusable.

Real-World Application

In threat assessment, we use these triads to categorize the impact of an event:

  • Ransomware: Primarily impacts Availability (through encryption) and Confidentiality (through exfiltration and extortion).
  • Website Defacement: Primarily impacts Integrity by altering the displayed content.
  • Industrial Control Systems (ICS): Often prioritize Availability and Integrity over Confidentiality, as uptime and safety are critical.
