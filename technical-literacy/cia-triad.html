The CIA Triad

The **CIA Triad** is the core foundational model designed to guide security policies and posture within an organization, balancing three essential pillars:

  • Confidentiality: Preventing unauthorized disclosure of information (ensuring secrets remain secret).
  • Integrity: Protecting data from unauthorized modification, tampering, or deletion (ensuring data remains trustworthy).
  • Availability: Ensuring authorized users have reliable, timely access to data and systems (ensuring uptime and accessibility).

The DAD Triad - Incident Impact

When threat events occur, analysts assess the damage using the **DAD Triad**, which represents the direct, adversarial opposites of the CIA Triad:

  • Disclosure (vs. Confidentiality): Unauthorized parties read or exfiltrate private data (e.g., database theft, breach of customer records).
  • Alteration (vs. Integrity): Data is modified or falsified (e.g., altering financial records, tampering with code repositories).
  • Destruction/Denial (vs. Availability): Systems or data are deleted, corrupted, or blocked (e.g., wiping servers, locking files, DDoS attacks).

Threats to Confidentiality & Integrity

CTI operations categorize adversary goals based on which security pillar they target:

  • Confidentiality Violations: Adversaries deploy spy implants, intercept network traffic (MitM), or run phishing campaigns targeting corporate intellectual property.
  • Integrity Violations: Threat groups target software update servers to insert backdoors (supply chain attacks), deface customer websites, or inject false entries into threat databases.

Threats to Availability

Availability attacks target business operations directly, seeking to disrupt communication or force operations offline:

  • Ransomware: Encrypts operational data and backups, creating immediate denial-of-service to internal business platforms.
  • Wipers (Destructive Malware): Payloads designed purely to destroy system boot records and partition tables, leaving servers unbootable (e.g., WhisperGate, HermeticWiper).
  • DDoS Attacks: Overwhelming network bandwidth or application resources to deny access to external customers.

IT vs. OT Security Priorities

The priority ordering of the CIA triad differs dramatically depending on the operational domain:

  • IT (Information Technology): Prioritizes **Confidentiality** first (protecting user data and business secrets), followed by Integrity, then Availability.
  • OT (Operational Technology / ICS): Prioritizes **Availability** and safety first (ensuring assembly lines, power grids, and water systems remain operational), followed by Integrity, and lastly Confidentiality.
