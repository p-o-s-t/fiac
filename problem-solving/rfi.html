What is an RFI?

A Request for Information (RFI) is a formal process used to gather specific details that are currently missing from the intelligence picture.

RFIs bridge the gap between what we know and what we need to know to make a decision.

Generating RFIs

When an analyst identifies an intelligence gap, they generate an RFI. A good RFI should be:

  • Specific: Clearly define the information needed.
  • Actionable: Explain why the information is required.
  • Time-Bound: Include a realistic deadline for the response.

Answering RFIs

Receiving and answering RFIs is a core duty of an intelligence analyst. Key principles include:

  • Timeliness: Provide the answer when it's needed, not after the decision is made.
  • Clarity: Use BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front) to give the answer immediately.
  • Sourcing: Always cite the sources used to build the answer.
