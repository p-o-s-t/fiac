Threat Mitigation & Courses of Action

A Course of Action (COA) in threat intelligence is a set of defensive recommendations designed to prevent, mitigate, or disrupt threat actor activities before or during an attack.

Rather than reacting to infections after they happen, COA development shifts focus to proactive shields, hardening systems, and closing attack paths identified in adversary intelligence.

Adversary Path Analysis: MLCOA & MDCOA

When analyzing a threat campaign, intelligence analysts forecast the adversary’s movements by defining two primary routes:

  • Most Likely COA (MLCOA): The behavior and TTPs the adversary is statistically most likely to use, based on historic trends, known skills, and standard playbooks.
  • Most Dangerous COA (MDCOA): The worst-case scenario that would result in the highest business impact (e.g., destructive wiping of databases), regardless of its likelihood.

Time-Based Predictions & Warnings

For COAs to be actionable, they must include temporal metrics that define when security operations must act:

  • Early Warning Indicators: Detecting adversary preparations (e.g., typosquatting domain registration, targeted credential harvesting).
  • Imminence Indicators: Signs of imminent attack execution (e.g., active vulnerability scanning against target servers, target network pinging).
  • Response Window: The estimated time defenders have to deploy counter-COAs before impact occurs.

Defensive Counter-COAs

Defensive options are selected to match the stage of the attack and the organization's capabilities, following NIST SP 800-160 guidance:

  • Block / Restrict: Blocking malicious IPs, domains, or hashes via EDR, firewall, and mail filters.
  • Isolate: Separating targeted assets from the core network to prevent lateral movement.
  • Decoy / Deceive: Route scanning activity to honeypots to monitor attacker methodologies.
  • Harden: Applying specific patches, disabling unused protocols, or altering user access privileges.

Operationalizing COAs

COA recommendations must be integrated directly into security operations to ensure rapid execution:

  • Playbook Integration: Translating COAs into EDR/SOAR playbooks that can be executed automatically or with a single click.
  • Patching Prioritization: Utilizing threat intelligence to override standard patching schedules and deploy hotfixes for actively exploited CVEs.
  • Red Team Validation: Simulating predicted adversary COAs to test the effectiveness of deployed counter-COAs.
