What is COA Development?

Course of Action (COA) development involves predicting an adversary's future behavior and developing defensive strategies to counter those moves.

Intelligence analysts help decision-makers choose the best path forward based on threat assessments.

Predicting Adversary Paths

Analysts typically identify two primary paths an adversary might take:

  • Most Likely COA (MLCOA): The behavior the adversary is most likely to exhibit based on their history, TTPs, and current situation.
  • Most Dangerous COA (MDCOA): The behavior that would cause the most significant impact or damage to the organization, even if it is less likely.

Time-Based Predictions

Predictions are more valuable when they include a temporal component. Analysts use "Time-Left-To-Act" indicators:

  • Early Warning: Identifying the first signs of preparation (e.g., domain registration).
  • Imminence: Recognizing indicators that an attack is about to happen (e.g., increased scanning).
  • Response Window: Defining the amount of time defenders have to implement a counter-COA.
