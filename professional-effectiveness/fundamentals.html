What is Cyber Threat Intelligence?

Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) is the collection, processing, and analysis of data about cyber threats to understand an adversary's motivations, targets, and attack behaviors.

It's not just data (like a list of IP addresses); it's evidence-based knowledge that helps organizations make informed security decisions.

Why do we need CTI?

  • Proactive Defense: Shift from reactive to predictive security.
  • Reduced Risk: Identify threats before they impact the business.
  • Better Decisions: Provide high-level context for leadership.
  • Resource Optimization: Focus defense on the most relevant threats.

Types of Intelligence

  • Strategic: High-level trends and geopolitical context for decision-makers.
  • Operational: Details about specific attacks and adversary capabilities.
  • Tactical: Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs) used by actors.
  • Technical: Specific indicators (IOCs) like IPs, MD5 hashes, and domains.
