Protecting Information

CTI often contains sensitive information about an organization's defenses or ongoing investigations. Data marking ensures that this information is shared safely and securely.

The most common standard for CTI is the Traffic Light Protocol (TLP).

Traffic Light Protocol (TLP)

  • TLP:RED: For the recipients' eyes only. No further sharing.
  • TLP:AMBER: Shared within the recipients' organization and its clients who need to know.
  • TLP:GREEN: Shared within the recipients' community or sector.
  • TLP:CLEAR: For public consumption. No restrictions on sharing.

Permissible Actions Protocol (PAP)

While TLP focuses on who can see information, PAP focuses on what a recipient can do with it.

PAP helps ensure that intelligence is used effectively without accidentally alerting an adversary (e.g., "PAP:RED" might mean "do not block these IPs yet").
