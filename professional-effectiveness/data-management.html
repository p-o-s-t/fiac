Threat Intelligence Platforms (TIP)

A TIP is a central repository used to aggregate, correlate, and analyze threat data from multiple sources. It automates the collection and dissemination of indicators (IOCs).

  • Open Source: MISP (Malware Information Sharing Platform), OpenCTI.
  • Commercial: ThreatConnect, ThreatQuotient, Anomali.

Data Sources: Internal & External

Effective CTI relies on a diverse range of data sources to build a complete picture of the threat landscape.

  • Internal Sources: SIEM logs, EDR telemetry, incident response reports, and internal vulnerability scans.
  • External Sources: OSINT (blogs, Twitter), ISAC feeds, commercial data providers, and government alerts.

Analysts must document these sources to ensure reliability and track intelligence gaps.

Collection Frameworks

A Collection Framework helps analysts map their data sources back to their intelligence requirements (PIRs).

  • Source Mapping: Identifying which specific tool or feed provides the data needed to answer a PIR.
  • Gap Analysis: Identifying requirements that cannot currently be met with existing data sources.
  • Lifecycle Management: Regularly reviewing and updating collection plans to ensure they remain relevant.
