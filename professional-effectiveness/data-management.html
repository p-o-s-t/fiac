CTI Storage & Curation

Threat intelligence is generated at a velocity and volume that quickly overwhelms manual spreadsheets. Effective data management requires structured database repositories and platforms that ensure threat information is searchable, indexable, and correlated.

Analysts store threat data not just to keep a historical log, but to construct actionable relational links between files, hosts, network endpoints, and adversary profiles.

Internal vs. External Telemetry Curation

A comprehensive threat intelligence program balances telemetry collected from internal security platforms with feeds acquired from external organizations:

  • Internal Telemetry: Ingesting logs from EDR, SIEM, firewall access records, email servers, and corporate vulnerability scans.
  • External Telemetry: Ingesting commercial vendor feeds, open-source blacklists, government alerts (e.g., CISA), and ISAC sharing streams.
  • Metadata Curation: Tracking telemetry sources to evaluate feed reliability, true positive rates, and resource costs.

Threat Intelligence Platforms (TIPs)

A **TIP** acts as a central aggregator and correlation engine for security operations, supporting both open-source (MISP, OpenCTI) and commercial solutions.

  • Standardized Formats: Utilizing **STIX 2.1** (Structured Threat Information Expression) to define indicators, actors, TTPs, and campaigns as relational JSON objects.
  • Sharing Protocols: Deploying **TAXII** (Trusted Automated Exchange of Intelligence Information) to automate the machine-to-machine exchange of STIX packages.
  • Correlation: Automatically linking a newly observed IP to historic campaigns and malware families registered in the platform.

Collection Management & Mapping

To prevent collecting data that does not serve the organization, analysts deploy a **Collection Management** strategy to map sources back to active requirements:

  • Source Mapping: Documenting which security tools or feeds are responsible for gathering data to address specific PIRs.
  • Gap Analysis: Formally identifying PIRs that cannot be answered due to a lack of telemetry, prompting new tool acquisitions or feed subscriptions.
  • Feeds Auditing: Regularly auditing sources to retire redundant or low-fidelity feeds that produce high false-positive rates.

Threat Data Lifecycle & Quality Control

Data stored in a TIP decays over time. CTI operations utilize data pipelines to enforce automated quality control:

  • Deduplication: Merging overlapping records of the same indicator from different sources to maintain a clean database.
  • Validation: Cross-checking ingested indicators against whitelist databases to prevent blocking critical services (e.g., DNS providers, cloud APIs).
  • Indicator Aging: Implementing aging pipelines that lower indicator confidence scores or delete them entirely once their active duration expires.
