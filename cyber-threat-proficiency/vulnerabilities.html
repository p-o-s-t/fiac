The CVE Process

Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) is a list of publicly disclosed cybersecurity vulnerabilities. Each CVE entry is assigned a unique identification number by a CVE Numbering Authority (CNA).

The National Vulnerability Database (NVD), maintained by NIST, synchronizes with the CVE list and provides additional analysis, such as CVSS scores and mapping to CWE (Common Weakness Enumeration).

CVSS: Common Vulnerability Scoring System

CVSS provides a numerical score reflecting the severity of a vulnerability. It is composed of three metric groups:

  • Base: Represents the intrinsic qualities of a vulnerability.
  • Temporal: Reflects characteristics that change over time (e.g., exploit availability).
  • Environmental: Tailors the score to a specific user's environment.

Scores range from 0.0 to 10.0, with 10.0 being the most severe.

EPSS: Exploit Prediction Scoring System

While CVSS measures severity, EPSS estimates the probability that a vulnerability will be exploited in the wild within the next 30 days.

Using EPSS helps organizations prioritize patching based on actual threat data rather than just the theoretical impact of a vulnerability.

CISA KEV List

The Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog is maintained by CISA. It lists vulnerabilities that have been confirmed to be exploited in the wild.

For many federal agencies and regulated industries, patching vulnerabilities on the KEV list is a mandatory requirement within specified timeframes.
