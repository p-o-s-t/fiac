Dark Web Forums and Marketplaces

The Dark Web is home to numerous underground forums and marketplaces where cybercriminals trade illicit goods and services.

These platforms facilitate the exchange of stolen data, malware, exploit kits, and other specialized tools used in cyberattacks.

Initial Access Brokers (IABs)

Initial Access Brokers (IABs) are specialized actors who gain unauthorized access to an organization's network and then sell that access to other cybercriminals, such as ransomware operators.

By purchasing access, attackers can bypass the initial stages of an attack and move directly to post-exploitation, significantly reducing the time and effort required to conduct a successful campaign.

Infostealer Logs

Infostealers are malware designed to steal sensitive information, such as login credentials, browser cookies, and cryptocurrency wallet data from infected systems. Examples include:

  • RedLine Stealer: One of the most common infostealers, often distributed through cracked software and malicious advertisements.
  • Vidar: A modular infostealer that can capture data from a wide range of applications.
  • Lumma Stealer: A relatively new infostealer that has gained popularity for its effectiveness and ease of use.

The stolen data is often compiled into "logs" and sold in bulk on criminal marketplaces.

Exploit Development as a Service

Cybercriminals also offer Exploit Development as a Service (EDaaS), where specialized developers create and sell custom exploits for zero-day vulnerabilities or unpatched software.

This allows less technically skilled attackers to use sophisticated exploits in their campaigns, further lowering the barrier to entry for cybercrime.
