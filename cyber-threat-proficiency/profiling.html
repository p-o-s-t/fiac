Who is Targeting Us?

A threat actor is an individual or group that performs malicious activities in cyberspace. Understanding who is behind an attack is key to predicting their next move.

Profiling involves identifying an actor's motivations, capabilities, and intent.

Types of Threat Actors

  • Nation-State (APT): Highly skilled, well-funded groups working for a government. Focus on espionage or sabotage.
  • Cybercriminals: Motivated by financial gain. Often use ransomware or phishing.
  • Hacktivists: Motivated by political or social causes. Often use DDoS or website defacement.
  • Insider Threats: Employees or contractors with authorized access who cause harm (intentional or not).

Adversary Profiling

Profiling helps defenders answer critical questions:

  • What are their primary targets?
  • What tools and malware do they prefer?
  • How sophisticated are their operations?
  • What is their typical attack pattern (TTPs)?
