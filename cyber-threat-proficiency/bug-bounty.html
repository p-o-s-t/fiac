Responsible Disclosure

Responsible Disclosure (or Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure) is the ethical practice of reporting a vulnerability to the software vendor before making it public.

This allows the vendor to fix the vulnerability and release a patch, protecting users and preventing exploitation by malicious actors.

Bug Bounty Platforms

Bug bounty platforms connect organizations with security researchers. Popular platforms include:

  • HackerOne: A leading platform that hosts bug bounty programs for major companies and government agencies.
  • Bugcrowd: Another prominent platform that utilizes a crowdsourced security model to identify vulnerabilities.

These platforms manage the reporting, triage, and reward process for security researchers.

Benefits to Defense

Bug bounty programs offer several advantages for an organization's defense:

  • Reduced Attack Surface: Continuous testing by thousands of researchers helps identify and close vulnerabilities before attackers find them.
  • Cost-Effective: Organizations pay for results (valid bugs found) rather than for time spent by traditional penetration testers.
  • Diverse Expertise: Access to a global community of researchers with a wide range of specialized skills and perspectives.
