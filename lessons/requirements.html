What is a Requirement?

Intelligence begins with a question. A requirement is a statement of information needed by a decision-maker to reduce uncertainty.

Without clear requirements, an intelligence team can quickly become overwhelmed by data and lose focus on what matters most.

Priority Intelligence Requirements (PIRs)

PIRs are the most critical questions facing an organization. They are often high-level and drive the entire collection process.

Example: "What threat actors are currently targeting the financial sector in our region?"

Specific Intelligence Requirements (SIRs)

SIRs break down PIRs into smaller, more manageable questions that can be answered through collection and analysis.

Example: "What malware families are associated with the APT groups identified in our PIR?"
