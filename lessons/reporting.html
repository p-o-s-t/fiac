Communicating Intelligence

Intelligence is only valuable if it is communicated effectively to those who need to act on it. Communication is the final phase of the intelligence lifecycle.

A good product is clear, concise, and focused on the bottom line.

DCWF Alignment: KSA 3022 | Task: 2617

BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

Place your most important findings and recommendations at the very beginning of your communication.

Decision-makers are often busy and may not read beyond the first page. BLUF ensures they see the key message immediately.

Analytical Writing Tips

  • Be Objective: Avoid bias and stick to the evidence.
  • Use Estimative Language: Express levels of confidence (e.g., "high confidence," "likely," "possibly").
  • Know Your Audience: Tailor the technical depth to the recipient (e.g., CEO vs. SOC Analyst).
  • Keep it Concise: Avoid unnecessary jargon and wordiness.
