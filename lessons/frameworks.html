Structuring Intelligence

Frameworks provide a structured way to analyze and communicate complex cyber threats. They help analysts organize data and speak a common language.

Commonly used frameworks include the Cyber Kill Chain, the Diamond Model, and MITRE ATT&CK.

DCWF Alignment: KSA 3582, 3446

The Diamond Model

This model analyzes a single event by identifying four interconnected elements:

  • Adversary: Who is behind the attack?
  • Infrastructure: What systems are they using?
  • Capability: What tools and techniques are they employing?
  • Victim: Who are they targeting?

MITRE ATT&CK

ATT&CK stands for Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge.

It is a globally-accessible knowledge base of adversary behaviors based on real-world observations. It helps defenders understand how attacks happen.
