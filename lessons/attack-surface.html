The Defender's Challenge

The Attack Surface is the total sum of all points where an unauthorized user can try to enter data to or extract data from an environment.

Maintaining awareness of your attack surface is critical for prioritizing defense.

DCWF Alignment: KSA 3137, 3630

External Attack Surface

This includes everything an outsider can see and potentially attack from the internet:

  • Public-facing servers and websites.
  • Domain names and subdomains.
  • Open ports and services.
  • Publicly exposed APIs.
  • Social media and employee information (OSINT).

Attack Surface Management (ASM)

ASM is the continuous process of discovering, analyzing, and monitoring an organization's external-facing assets.

By understanding what is visible, organizations can proactively address vulnerabilities before an attacker can exploit them.
