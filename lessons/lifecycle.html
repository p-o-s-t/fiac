The Intelligence Process

Intelligence is not a one-time event; it's a cyclic process. It begins with a need and ends with a finished product that informs the next round of questions.

Commonly referred to as the Intelligence Lifecycle, it ensures intelligence is relevant, timely, and actionable.

DCWF Alignment: KSA 3582 | Task: 2115

1. Planning and Direction

Define Priority Intelligence Requirements (PIRs). What do decision-makers need to know?

Setting clear goals prevents "intelligence for intelligence's sake" and focuses resources on the most critical threats.

2. Collection and Processing

Collection: Gathering raw data from multiple sources (OSINT, SIGINT, HUMINT, etc.).

Processing: Converting raw data into a usable format—translating documents, decrypting files, or normalizing logs.

3. Analysis and Dissemination

Analysis: Turning processed data into intelligence by adding context, identifying patterns, and drawing conclusions.

Dissemination: Delivering the right information to the right person at the right time in the right format (e.g., a report or a dashboard).
